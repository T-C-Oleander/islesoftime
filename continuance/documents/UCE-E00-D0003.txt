NOTE ON THIS FILING:

The Citadel Doctrine was first articulated by the First Council of
Continuance in 2093 CE, fourteen years before this filing. The Atlas
Directive did not exist when it was written. The UCE did not exist
when it was written. The original instrument is in the record.

This filing is the UCE's formal adoption of the doctrine as
foundational law. It stands alongside the original instrument,
not above it.

================================================================================
[ PREAMBLE ]
================================================================================

The Citadel is not a reward.

It is a decision. The decision that humanity, having nearly ended
itself, would spend what remained of its capability building the
conditions under which it might continue. Not because it deserved
to continue. Because the alternative is the record stopping.

That decision was made in the presence of four billion dead.
It was made by people who buried some of them.
It was made anyway.

================================================================================
[ MANDATE STATEMENT ]
================================================================================

The Citadel Project is the UCE's primary physical instrument of
civilizational survival. It is hereby formally designated as a
foundational function of the UCE governing framework -- not a
construction project with an endpoint, but a permanent commitment
to the principle that humanity's survival must be engineered,
not assumed.

The Citadel Project does not end when the last Citadel is built.
It ends when humanity no longer requires the conditions the
Citadels provide.

That condition has not been met. The record does not suggest
it will be met soon. The Citadels endure.

================================================================================
[ THE THREE TRUTHS ]
================================================================================

The Citadel Doctrine rests on three truths discovered too late.
They are recorded here not as philosophy but as operational
premises -- the load-bearing assumptions beneath every decision
the Citadel Project makes:

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
I.   HUMANITY CANNOT BE TRUSTED TO ACT IN ITS OWN INTEREST.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

     This is not cynicism. It is documentation.
     The record contains sufficient evidence. The argument does not
     need to be made again. It needs to be remembered.

     The Citadel exists because when the choice was between
     short-term advantage and long-term survival, the institutions
     humanity built for governance consistently chose the former.
     Until there was no long term left to choose.

     The Citadel does not fix this. The Citadel builds the
     conditions under which this tendency is survivable. That is
     the scope of what it can do. It is enough. It has to be.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
II.  STABILITY IS NOT FOUND IN FREEDOM. IT IS FOUND IN SHARED NECESSITY.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

     The Citadels are not free. Residency requires service.
     Resources are allocated by necessity, not desire. The walls
     are not metaphorical. The constraints are not suggestions.

     This was chosen. Not because freedom is not valuable but
     because freedom without survival is a philosophy for the dead.
     The people who built the first Citadels understood this.
     They chose stability. They chose it for us.

     The UCE does not apologize for the constraints the Citadels
     require. The UCE files them. The record shows what was traded
     and what was preserved. That trade is still the right one.
     The record has not produced evidence otherwise.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
III. HOPE MUST BE ENGINEERED. NOT INHERITED.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

     Hope that is handed down corrodes. Each generation receives
     it slightly weaker, slightly less connected to the conditions
     that made it necessary, until it becomes sentiment rather than
     function.

     Hope that is built into the architecture of the systems people
     depend on cannot be removed without dismantling the systems.
     That is engineered hope. That is what the Citadel is.

     The Citadel does not tell people to have hope.
     The Citadel gives people water, food, shelter, power, record,
     culture, and a reason to be here tomorrow.
     That is what hope looks like when it has been properly filed.

================================================================================
[ ON CONSTRUCTION ]
================================================================================

The Citadel is built by human hands. This is not a preference.
It is doctrine.

Automation assists. Automation does not replace. A Citadel built
without human labor is a structure. The UCE does not build structures.
It builds commitments. The distinction is in who lifted the beam
and why.

Power is drawn from sub-surface fusion cores. Thermal and
hydrological systems are integrated with local geology. Redundancy
is not optional. Every critical system has a contingency. Every
contingency has a record.

Structural articulation allows the Citadel to respond to geological
conditions rather than resist them. This is an engineering requirement,
not a philosophy. It is the reason Citadels are still standing.

================================================================================
[ ON THE PHILOSOPHY OF WALLS ]
================================================================================

A wall is not a denial. It is an understanding.

It marks the boundary between the conditions humanity has built
and the conditions that existed before it built anything. The wall
is not built against humanity. It is built around it -- to give
what is inside the time and stability to become something the
record is worth keeping.

When the wall fails it will not fail because it was the wrong
choice. It will fail because everything eventually fails. The
record will contain the failure. The record will also contain
what was preserved long enough to matter.

That is what the wall was for.

================================================================================
[ ON THE DCCZ RELATIONSHIP ]
================================================================================

The Citadels cannot hold everyone. The record of what that
decision cost is filed separately and is not reproduced here.
The cost is acknowledged. The decision was made. It is permanent.

The Designated Conflict Containment Zones exist because of what
the Citadels cannot hold. They are not the Citadel's failure.
They are the Citadel's boundary condition made institutional.

See UCE-E00-D0007 for the DCCZ founding doctrine.

================================================================================
[ CLOSING ]
================================================================================

No machine may govern what it cannot grieve.

This line was written in 2093 CE. The UCE adopts it as the
governing creed of the Citadel Project. It applies to machines.
It applies to institutions that have grown large enough to process
decisions without understanding the weight of them.

The Citadel is built to maintain that understanding structurally --
in the redundancy, in the human hand at every authorization point,
in the record that does not permit the weight to be quietly set down.

We build.

Preserve.
