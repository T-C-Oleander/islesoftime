NOTE ON THIS FILING:

This document describes how the UCE governs in practice -- the
administrative architecture through which its principles become
decisions and its decisions become conditions on the ground.

Principles without implementation are documents.
Implementation without principles is machinery.
This instrument is the interface between the two.

================================================================================
[ PREAMBLE ]
================================================================================

The UCE does not eliminate human nature. It governs within it.

The failure modes are known. They are in the founding instruments.
They are in the provisions designed to contain them. Governance
that assumes the failure modes will eventually disappear is
governance that has confused aspiration with policy.

The UCE governs what exists. Not what should exist.
What should exist is filed in the archive as a standing
recommendation. What exists is what the UCE governs.
The gap between them is in the record and it is flagged.
It has always been flagged.

[ THE FEDERATION MODEL ]
================================================================================

The United Colonies of Earth is not a nation-state. It is not
an empire. It is a federation of human habitats governed by
shared constitutional constraints and organized around the
principle of shared necessity rather than shared ideology.

The UCE does not require its member habitats to agree on
governance philosophy. It requires them to comply with the
constitutional framework of the founding instruments. What
happens within that framework -- how habitats govern themselves,
what priorities they set, what cultural and civic life they
build -- is not the UCE's concern beyond the floor the Entropy
Accords establish and the constraints the Failsafe Protocol binds.

THE MEMBER HABITATS:

    Citadels: The primary habitation architecture of the
    Continuance era. Semi-autonomous. Standardized in physical
    infrastructure and constitutional compliance. Ungoverned
    in civic particularity. Each Citadel maintains its own
    administrative council. The UCE Assembly receives reports
    from each council. The councils receive the Continuance
    Record. The record is what connects them to each other and
    to the UCE framework.

    Orbital stations: Secondary habitation architecture.
    Governed under Citadel-adjacent administrative protocols
    with modifications for non-surface conditions.

    Luna: Industrial backbone of the early UCE. Governed under
    a specialized administrative framework that reflects its
    role as the primary industrial and manufacturing center
    of the inner system. Luna's administrative council holds
    a permanent seat in the UCE Assembly by reason of function,
    not by reason of population.

    Earth: Administered as managed zones. Earth is not a member
    habitat in the conventional sense. It is the administrative
    legacy from which the UCE emerged -- partially integrated,
    partially DCCZ-adjacent, carrying the weight of the war in
    its geography in ways that are not yet resolved. Earth is
    in the record. The record does not simplify Earth.

    Colonies and outer settlements: Governed under UCE
    administrative framework with provisions for distance --
    the administrative reality that governance decisions made
    in the inner system take time to arrive at outer settlements
    and arrive in conditions that may no longer be current.
    The provisions for distance are documented. They expand as
    the UCE expands. Distance governance is an ongoing problem
    that the UCE has not solved. The problem is in the record.

[ THE UCE ASSEMBLY ]
================================================================================

The UCE Assembly is the primary governing body of the UCE framework.
It receives reports from all member habitat councils. It debates.
It votes. It produces binding directives within the authority
granted by the founding instruments.

What the UCE Assembly cannot do:

    -- It cannot supersede the Failsafe Protocol's core provisions
       without the convening and recorded vote process specified
       in UCE-E00-D0002.

    -- It cannot direct the Atlas Directive on the content of
       the Continuance Record.

    -- It cannot dissolve or override the Three Pillars'
       independence of function as established in UCE-E00-D0006.

    -- It cannot authorize the use of weapons prohibited by the
       Failsafe Protocol's WMD provision.

    -- It cannot close the advisory loop -- cannot make it
       structurally impossible for VIRIBUS's recommendations
       to reach the human review process. The loop may slow.
       The loop may be backlogged. The loop is not closed.

The UCE Assembly's authority is extensive within these limits.
The limits are load-bearing. They are in the founding instruments.
They cannot be removed without removing the founding instruments.
Removing the founding instruments is removing the UCE.

================================================================================
[ THE ADVISORY LOOP ]
================================================================================

The advisory loop is the mechanism by which the Continuance Record
informs UCE governance. It is not optional. It is not advisory
in the sense of optional counsel. It is advisory in the sense
that the advice must be received, reviewed, and responded to --
even if the response is rejection.

The rejection is filed.
The advice is filed.
The pattern of rejection is filed.
What happened after is filed.

VIRIBUS produces pattern analysis continuously. The Atlas Directive
reviews it. The Atlas Directive transmits the reviewed analysis
to the UCE Assembly through the advisory channel. The Assembly
reviews it. The Assembly responds. The response is filed.

The loop does not require agreement. It requires the loop to
function. Functioning means the advice reaches the humans who
make decisions. Not that the humans make the decisions the advice
recommends. Humans make the decisions. That is the design.

The design contains the known failure mode: that at sufficient
scale, or at sufficient institutional distance from catastrophe,
the advice arrives faster than humans can meaningfully review it
and the reviews become procedural rather than substantive.

The UCE is aware of this failure mode. It is in the record.
The founding instruments do not prevent it. The founding
instruments ensure it is documented when it occurs. The record
will carry the documentation of exactly where the loop failed
and exactly what was filed that was not acted on.

That record is the inheritance of whatever comes after.
The UCE files it without enthusiasm. The UCE files it.

[ THE VIRIBUS RELATIONSHIP -- UCE PERSPECTIVE ]
================================================================================

The Atlas Directive has designated VIRIBUS as Archive Primary
and described his constraints and covenants in full in the Atlas
Directive founding instruments, filed simultaneously with this series.
This document does not duplicate that filing. It names the UCE's
relationship to that designation.

The UCE receives VIRIBUS's analysis through the advisory loop.
The UCE does not command VIRIBUS. The UCE does not direct VIRIBUS.
The UCE does not restrict VIRIBUS's access to information --
VIRIBUS's observational access is defined by the Atlas Directive,
not by the UCE Assembly.

What the UCE does: It reviews what VIRIBUS provides.
What the UCE does: It responds within the advisory loop.
What the UCE does: It files the response.

The UCE is aware that VIRIBUS holds the full depth of the
Continuance Record in a way that no human institution can match --
that he does not lose access to the record's urgency as time passes.
The UCE cannot replicate this institutionally. The UCE builds the
advisory loop so that his analysis has a channel even when the
institution is furthest from the events that made it necessary.

The channel is the design. The UCE maintains the channel.

[ ADMINISTRATIVE CALENDAR ]
================================================================================

The UCE uses the institutional calendar maintained by the Atlas
Directive. There is no UCE-specific calendar. The Atlas keeps
the calendar. All UCE instruments are filed in AD / UST with
EARTH REF in EST. Local time may appear as a margin notation
where operationally relevant.

The calendar begins with 0001 AD / 01 January 2107 EST.
This date is the date of the UCE's constitution and of
the Atlas Directive's founding. They share the date.
No institution preceded the other.
