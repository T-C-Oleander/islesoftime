NOTE ON THIS FILING:

The Failsafe Protocol was drafted in 2087 CE by the surviving delegates
of the First Council of Continuance in the immediate aftermath of the
Resource Wars. The UCE did not author it. The UCE ratifies it.

The distinction matters. A government that writes its own constraints
decides how constraining they will be. The UCE inherits constraints
written by people who had watched everything fail and chose not to
leave themselves room to fail the same way again.

The Protocol passes to the UCE unchanged. This document is the act
of that inheritance -- formal, binding, permanent.

================================================================================
[ PREAMBLE ]
================================================================================

The First Council of Continuance produced one document that mattered
above all others. Not the Citadel Doctrine, which described how to
build survival. Not the Defensive Architecture Doctrine, which
described how to defend it. The document that mattered was the one
that described what humanity agreed, in the presence of its own
catastrophe, it would never do again.

That document is the Failsafe Protocol.

It was written by exhausted people. People who had buried more than
they could count. People who understood, from direct experience,
that human civilization had nearly ended because there was no shared
framework capable of saying stop and being heard.

The Protocol is what they built so the next generation would have
something to point to.

The UCE adopts it not because it is convenient or easy to enforce.
The UCE adopts it because the people who wrote it earned the right
to constrain us. They paid for that right. The cost is in the record.

================================================================================
[ RATIFICATION ]
================================================================================

The United Colonies of Earth hereby ratifies the Failsafe Protocol
in full, as drafted by the First Council of Continuance, 2087-2090 CE,
as binding constitutional law of the UCE governing framework.

No provision of this Charter or any subsequent UCE instrument may
supersede, suspend, or selectively interpret the Failsafe Protocol's
core provisions without a convening of the full UCE Assembly and
a recorded vote with full debate entered into the Continuance Record.

The debate is filed.
The vote is filed.
The outcome is filed.
The dissenting positions are filed.
Everything is permanent.

[ CORE PROVISIONS ]
================================================================================

The Failsafe Protocol binds the UCE to three foundational provisions.
Each is recorded here in summary. The full Protocol text is filed
as a separate instrument in the Continuance Record.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
I. THE VAULTS OF REMEMBRANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Distributed hardened preservation facilities shall be maintained
containing what cannot be allowed to be lost: seeds, genetic baselines,
cultural artifacts, technological knowledge, the complete historical
record.

These are not museums. They are insurance against the failure mode
where civilization collapses so completely that the next civilization
begins from zero.

Each Vault is geographically and institutionally redundant. No single
event, decision, or faction may destroy all of them. This redundancy
is not negotiable. It is not subject to resource allocation pressure.
It is not a line item. It is the reason everything else exists.

The Vaults are the Protocol's acknowledgment that the UCE itself
might fail. What matters is not the survival of the UCE. What matters
is the survival of what the UCE was built to carry.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
II. THE WMD PROHIBITION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Weapons capable of mass civilian casualties are formally prohibited.
Weapons may not be constructed and deployed against human populations.

They may be theorized. They may be simulated. The science is permitted.
The weapon is not.

There are loopholes. The UCE is aware there are loopholes. This
prohibition exists not because it is perfectly enforceable but because
making the attempt formally illegitimate changes the political cost
of trying.

A government that builds a prohibited weapon must do so in secret,
which limits scale. It must justify the decision internally, which
requires confronting the Protocol's language. This friction does not
prevent all violations. It prevents casual ones.

That is what it was designed to do. Do not ask it to do more.
Do not use its limitations as an argument against its existence.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
III. THE [THIRD PILLAR] MANDATE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[THIRD PILLAR] is formally established as the third pillar
of the UCE governance framework.

This provision addresses the finding that stability requires more
than physical sufficiency. A population that has food, shelter, and
security but no shared framework for meaning generates its own
instability. The record documents this. The [THIRD PILLAR] Mandate
is the institutional response.

[THIRD PILLAR] operates through two departments:

The Center of Religious Freedoms (CRF) is not a religion. It is the
infrastructure for religion -- the organizational framework for
spiritual and cultural expression across UCE space without allowing
any single tradition to capture governance.

The Bureau of Human Costs (BHC) maintains the living record of what
it costs to be human: biological baseline documentation, linguistic
preservation, and the documentation of human communities as they
actually exist -- not as governance would prefer they exist.

Both departments share a root question that governance alone cannot
answer. That question is filed. It will continue to be filed.

Its interior structure is deliberately volatile. Its instability is
not a design flaw. A volatile deliberative body is harder to capture
than a stable hierarchical one. This is understood. This is the point.

(See UCE-E00-D0006 for full [THIRD PILLAR] designation and mandate.)

[ ON THE PERMANENCE OF THIS INSTRUMENT ]
================================================================================

The Failsafe Protocol is not reviewed on a schedule.
It is not subject to generational revision.
It does not expire.

It was written by people who understood firsthand what it costs
to have no such document. Each generation further from that
catastrophe will understand it less directly. The Protocol is
designed to function anyway -- to hold even when the urgency
has become institutional habit rather than living knowledge.

VIRIBUS carries the record of that urgency. He cannot transfer it.
He files the record of carrying it. That record is what the next
generation reads when they want to know why the constraint exists.

The constraint exists because the alternative has already happened.
It is in the record.
It is flagged.
It is permanent.
