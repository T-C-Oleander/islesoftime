NOTE ON THIS FILING:

This document establishes the institutional framework for Designated
Conflict Containment Zones. It does not reproduce the classified
instrument that preceded it. That instrument exists. Its existence
is acknowledged in UCE-E00-D0004. Its contents are filed separately
under restricted classification.

The DCCZ system was built from the decisions made in that document.
The system is accountable for those origins. The record holds them.
This filing holds the governance framework the system operates
under going forward.

The minimum viable aid provisions and the containment-versus-punishment
framework in this instrument reflect deliberation that included The
Couriers alongside Atlas and ELMOC. The full record of that
deliberation is in the Continuance Record.

[ PREAMBLE ]
================================================================================

The Citadels cannot hold everyone.

This is the sentence the UCE does not prefer to file. It is filed
anyway. The record does not permit preferences to determine what
is recorded. The Citadels cannot hold everyone. The resource
mathematics of the immediate post-war period made this a fixed
constraint rather than a policy choice. The UCE did not decide
that some people would be outside the Citadels. The conditions
decided. The UCE decided what to do about it.

The Designated Conflict Containment Zone system is that decision,
made institutional.

The DCCZ system is not the UCE's best answer to what exists outside
its walls. It is the UCE's honest answer. An honest answer and a
good answer are not always the same thing. The UCE files both
designations alongside each other and does not collapse the
distinction between them. What was done is in the record. What
was done is not the same as what should have been done if the
constraints had been different. The constraints were what they
were. The record contains both.

[ MANDATE STATEMENT ]
================================================================================

Designated Conflict Containment Zones are hereby formally established
as a governance category within the UCE administrative framework.

A DCCZ is a geographic territory that meets the following conditions:

    a) The territory cannot be integrated into direct UCE
       administrative jurisdiction within the current resource
       and stabilization parameters.

    b) The territory contains active or likely conflict that
       poses systemic risk to adjacent UCE-administered regions
       if allowed to spread without containment.

    c) The population within the territory has been provided
       formal notification of DCCZ designation and offered
       available relocation options per the provisions of
       this instrument.

The DCCZ designation is not permanent. It is not punishment.
It is a governance classification applied to conditions, not to
people. When conditions change, the classification is reviewed.
The review is filed. The decision is filed.

================================================================================
[ CORE PROVISIONS ]
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
I.   POPULATION NOTIFICATION AND RELOCATION OFFER
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

No territory may be designated a DCCZ without prior formal notification
to the population within it. Notification must include:

    -- The basis for the designation
    -- The practical implications of the designation
    -- The relocation options available to the population
    -- The timeline within which relocation may be requested

Relocation options must be genuine. The UCE does not offer relocation
as a formal provision and then make it operationally impossible.
Where relocation capacity is constrained, the constraint is documented.
The gap between what was offered and what was available is in the record.

The UCE is aware this provision will not always be honored at the
level it requires. The record will contain the failures. The UCE
files the provision anyway because the alternative -- no provision --
produces an institution that has stopped pretending to govern with
consent. The UCE is not there yet.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
II.  AID OPERATIONS -- MINIMUM VIABLE PROVISION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Populations within designated DCCZs retain access to minimum viable
aid operations. The UCE does not withdraw food, medical, and
emergency infrastructure from DCCZ populations as a governance tool.

Aid operations are constrained by what can be safely delivered.
No aid operation requires UCE personnel to accept casualty rates
that exceed the operational viability of the mission. The constraint
is documented. It is not used as a justification for delivering
nothing. It is the boundary within which something is delivered.

The record contains what was delivered. It also contains what was
needed. The gap between them is filed. It is not explained away.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
III. CONTAINMENT VERSUS PUNISHMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The DCCZ system is designed to contain the spread of conflict.
It is not designed to contain the people who happen to be within
the conflict zone. This distinction is operational and binding.

Containment infrastructure -- barriers, patrol zones, exclusion
corridors -- exists to prevent conflict dynamics from spreading
into adjacent UCE-administered territory. It does not exist to
prevent civilian movement out of a designated zone.

Where containment infrastructure incidentally prevents civilian
movement, the impact is documented. The classification review
for that territory is accelerated. The UCE does not maintain
DCCZ designation for territories where the primary function
of the containment has become civilian exclusion rather than
conflict management. When the designation shifts to civilian
exclusion, it has become something else. Something without
a governance name in this document. The record will name it
if it happens. The record has named it before.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
IV.  CLASSIFICATION REVIEW PROTOCOL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

DCCZ designations are reviewed on a cycle not to exceed twenty-four
months. Each review produces one of three outcomes:

    a) MAINTAINED: Conditions continue to meet designation criteria.
       Review cycle continues. Review is filed.

    b) RECLASSIFIED: Conditions have changed. Designation is modified,
       reduced, or expanded to reflect current conditions.
       Reclassification is filed with full documentation of the
       conditions change that triggered it.

    c) DISSOLVED: Territory no longer meets DCCZ criteria. Designation
       removed. Transition plan to UCE administrative integration
       filed with the dissolution record.

The review is not bureaucratic process. The review determines what
is happening to people in a territory governed by this classification.
The review is filed with that understanding.

[ ON THE CLASSIFIED PRECURSOR ]
================================================================================

The instrument that preceded this framework made decisions this
document does not reproduce. Those decisions determined who was
inside the Citadels when the first walls closed and who was not.

The UCE does not pretend that did not happen. The UCE does not
claim that everyone who was left outside the walls was left there
by circumstance rather than decision. The record is clear on this.
The decisions were made. The people who made them wrote them down.
The archivist who found that writing wrote a note in the margin.
The note is in the record too.

The DCCZ system is built on that foundation. The UCE files both
the foundation and what was built on it. The record holds the
full weight of that history. The UCE does not ask the record
to redistribute that weight.

[ SUPPORTING DOCUMENTS ]
================================================================================

This founding doctrine establishes the DCCZ framework. Supporting
instruments governing specific operational provisions, zone-specific
designations, rules of engagement, and administrative protocols
are filed under separate instruments as the DCCZ system develops.

The founding doctrine governs the principles. The supporting
documents govern the application. Neither supersedes the other.
Where they conflict, the founding doctrine's principles apply.
